How Temporary Fence Rental Works in Wisconsin

Temporary fence rental is a straightforward process. You call a local rental company, describe your site or event, and they deliver, install, and eventually remove the fencing. The rental fee covers the panels, delivery, setup, and pickup.

In Northeast Wisconsin — Green Bay, the Fox Valley, and Door County — construction sites and outdoor events are the primary applications. Wisconsin's construction season runs roughly April through November, which creates a concentrated period of demand for temporary construction fencing across the state.

Types of Temporary Fencing

Chain Link Fence Panels

The most common type for construction sites. Standard panels are 6 feet tall by 12 feet wide, made from galvanized steel chain link on a tube steel frame. They sit on weighted bases and require no ground penetration. Chain link panels provide genuine security, good visibility, and withstand Wisconsin winter conditions when needed for year-round projects.

Privacy Screen Fencing

Mesh privacy screens attach to chain link panels to block visibility into a construction site or event area. Required by some Wisconsin municipalities for demolition and renovation projects near public sidewalks. Also used at events to create defined VIP areas or backstage zones. Privacy screens act like a sail in wind, so additional ballast is typically needed.

Crowd Control Barricades

Steel interlocking barricades for pedestrian crowd management at events. Lighter and more portable than chain link panels. Used along race routes, at festival entry queues, and for directing foot traffic at outdoor events throughout Green Bay and the Fox Valley.

Drive Gates & Pedestrian Gates

Every fenced construction site needs at least one entry point. Drive gates allow equipment and vehicles to access the site. Pedestrian gates provide crew entry without opening the full site perimeter. Both include padlock hardware and integrate with standard chain link panel configurations.

Temporary Fence Rental Costs in Wisconsin

Temporary fence rental in Wisconsin starts at $3 per linear foot per month. The rate improves with larger footage and longer rental terms — a 500-foot construction site perimeter on a 6-month rental will cost meaningfully less per foot than a 100-foot short-term job. All-in pricing includes delivery, installation, and removal.

A typical construction site perimeter of 200 linear feet would start around $600 per month. Gates, privacy screens, and sandbag ballast are priced separately.

Factors that affect the total cost:

  • Total linear footage needed
  • Rental duration (longer terms often get better monthly rates)
  • Number and size of gates required
  • Whether privacy screens are needed
  • Delivery distance from the rental company's yard
  • Site terrain (uneven ground requires more ballast)

How Long Does Installation Take?

Installation time for temporary fencing depends primarily on the total linear footage and the number of gate openings. As a general guide for Wisconsin construction sites:

  • Under 150 feet: 45–90 minutes
  • 150–300 feet: 1.5–2.5 hours
  • 300–500 feet: 2.5–4 hours
  • 500+ feet: Half day or more depending on complexity

We schedule installation to be complete before your site opens or your event begins. For event fencing in Green Bay, we can often set up the evening before if the venue allows early access.

Temporary Fencing Requirements for Wisconsin Construction Sites

Wisconsin OSHA standards and local city codes govern temporary fencing requirements on construction sites. General rules that apply to most Green Bay and Fox Valley construction projects:

  • Sites adjacent to public sidewalks or streets typically require 6-foot perimeter fencing
  • Demolition projects in urban areas often require privacy screening to contain debris
  • Active excavation sites require secure perimeter fencing to prevent unauthorized entry
  • Gate openings must allow emergency vehicle access in most city permit requirements

Choosing a Temporary Fence Rental Company in Wisconsin

The most important factors when choosing a temporary fence rental company in Northeast Wisconsin:

Local vs. National Operators

National temporary fence rental companies operate throughout Wisconsin but often route calls through distant call centers and may have limited local inventory. A local company can respond faster, provide same-day delivery, and offer more flexibility on rental terms. For construction sites in Green Bay, Appleton, and Door County, working with a local operator means faster setup when your project timeline shifts.

Full-Service vs. Drop-and-Go

Some companies drop panels and expect you to install them yourself. Full-service companies deliver, install, reconfigure as needed, and handle complete removal. For most construction sites, full-service is worth the premium — your crew's time is better spent on the project than on fence assembly.

Rental Term Flexibility

Construction timelines change. A rental company that charges penalty fees for extensions or requires rigid end dates will create friction as your project evolves. Month-to-month rental terms with easy extensions are the standard for construction site fencing in Wisconsin.
